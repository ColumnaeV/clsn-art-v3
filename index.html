Being a Digital Artist

If I'm gonna be honest, digital art was never really my thing. I mean sure, we all enjoy saving the moments of our lives through the photos we take on our phones, but how often do we look back on them?

I guess my first experience to digital art had to have been my website development projects, as it required custom graphics to be made, but editing photos only came recently as one of my best mates, Austin Nguyen, introduced me into the world Photoshop. Over time I've developed various techniques of photo editing from Austin and others in the C&C Studios community, but now I've really set upon one. If you've haven't noticed it yet, it's this Chalk Line edit style of me that you see.

As much as my thoughts are scattered writing these HTML 'p tags', so are my styles of digital artwork. I hope you enjoy them. :)

Photoshopping with C&C Studios

One of the best resources I've used for learning new methods of graphic editing is the C&C Studios community. Although the current team is rather quite niche, I've learned a lot from the team. With graphic design I've learned how to utilise and expand my knowledge of Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere, Lightroom, and Fireworks. With respect to photography, I still use the lens of an iPhone 8 Plus, however I've learned various techniques in conventional photography to reduce the time needed in Photoshop. My goal in starting C&C Studios was to help and encourage others in the exploration of digital art, but I didn't know that I would be a part of that group. If you're interested in C&C Studios, check it out!
